Whos your point guard?

by Ivan Agacer

Posted on November 7 at 3:00 PM

Whos better? Russell Wesbrook or Stephen Curry

It's virtually impossible to imagine a successful NBA team without great point guard play. Point guards are often the extension of a coach, responsible for enhancing the play of others offensively while also setting the tone defensively.

So, who's the best point guard in today's NBA: Stephen Curry? Chris Paul? Russell Westbrook? There's no easy answer and we all have a preference. But the question isn't who do we prefer, it's who's the best. Many factors -- from clutch play to leadership and beyond -- come into play in this debate. Perhaps a legendary point guard (who himself was once in a "best point guard" debate in his day) can solve the issue? NBA TV analyst, Hall of Famer and legendary Detroit Pistons point guard Isiah Thomas explains the best point guard thusly: "First thing you have to be able to do is take the game plan out of the coach's hand and execute it out on the floor. Do you have voice? If you don't have a voice then you can't lead the team," Thomas said. "Skill wise, you have to be able to score. If you can't score the basketball then you're a liability on the floor because your passing lanes are closed. "You also have to be able to make all the passes. Bounce pass, chest pass, over the head pass, pass off the dribble, pass with touch and pass with timing. At the end of a game you have to be a decent foul shooter because the ball is going to be in your hands. Defensively, unlike a big man, you find yourself in every situation. You're getting posted up, you have to guard pick and roll, you have to guard one-on-one and mid-range. Therefore, you have to guard 94 feet."
